About

The Lehigh University Art Galleries commissioned a museum-grade looking guide for their Spring 2026 exhibition Color, Culture and Chemistry: Kiln and Glaze Technology in Chinese Ceramics. The brief was to create an accessible, visually compelling self-guided tour for K–12 visitors - one that balanced scholarly content with engaging design and interactive looking prompts. The process moved from competitive research and moodboarding through hand sketching to a fully realized printed publication.

01 - Ideation & Moodboard

Before touching layout software, the process started with competitive analysis - collecting existing museum looking guides from institutions including MoMA, the Saint Louis Art Museum, and smaller gallery programs. Key patterns emerged: alternating accent colors, generous negative space, zoomed close-up cutouts, activity boxes, and accessible typography. These became the guiding design principles.

Moodboard - competitive audit of museum K–12 looking guides

Across every reference, two patterns stood out: strong alternating color fields that create visual rhythm, and "A Closer Look" zoomed-in cutouts that reward careful observation.

From the moodboard, a set of design decisions crystallized: two main alternating colors (warm brown and cream), Chinese character dynasty labels as typographic anchors, transparent-background object photography, and "Looking Prompts" as the primary interactive device. The guide needed to feel both educational and genuinely beautiful - something a visitor would want to hold onto after the exhibition.

02 - Sketching

With the design language defined, the next step was mapping the full publication layout through hand sketching. Each panel of the folded brochure was roughed out: the cover with a large ceramic object and vertical "Looking Guide" spine text, the table of contents spread, and the individual artifact pages - each following a consistent front/back structure with the artifact large, "A Closer Look" cutouts on the right column, and Looking Prompts anchoring the bottom.

Thumbnail Sketches

Mapping every panel before opening InDesign

Sixteen thumbnail sketches mapped the front cover, inside spreads, and back cover - including alternate cover compositions, dynasty character placement, and multiple artifact page structures. Key decisions made at this stage: a vertical "Looking Guide" label on the cover spine, the "A Closer Look" column running along the right edge of odd pages, and the LUAG logo anchoring the bottom left of the front and back covers.

Font candidates were also identified during sketching: Trade Gothic Next was selected for its balance of editorial authority and readability at small sizes.

03 - Final Design

The final publication is an 8-panel folded brochure structured around three thematic sections - Early Decoration, Colored Glazes, and Influences of Trade - each anchored to a specific Chinese dynasty. Every spread pairs a large artifact photograph with dynasty typography in Chinese characters, "A Closer Look" close-up cutouts, and Looking Prompts designed to encourage observation rather than test knowledge. The warm brown and cream palette creates a cohesive visual identity that references the earth and glaze tones of the ceramics themselves.
