Course Registration

Role UI Designer
Duration 3 months
Tools Figma
Type Case Study
UI/UX Design

About

University course registration is one of the most consequential and most frustrating systems students interact with every semester. This project set out to understand why that friction exists and redesign the experience from the ground up through rigorous user research, iterative prototyping, and validated design decisions.

This one started with me. When I first arrived at SUTD as an exchange student, navigating the new portal was genuinely disorienting. I didn't know where to start or what I was looking at. That experience stuck, and it became the lens I used to approach every decision in this redesign.

01 - User Research

Survey · 33 Students

84% found the original system difficult

A baseline survey of 33 students showed 84% rated the enrolment process as "Difficult" or "Very Difficult" on first use. This data made the case for a fundamental rethink and anchored every design decision that followed.

84% of students rated the course enrolment process as "Difficult" or "Very Difficult" on their first attempt - a clear signal that the problem was worth solving from the ground up.

User Map

Mapping the stakeholder landscape

We mapped the full range of people the registration system touches: undergraduate and graduate students, exchange students, faculty, staff, and parents. This helped surface who experiences the system differently and where friction concentrates most.

User Persona

Danny Love: a typical fourth-year student

Danny is a fourth-year ASD pillar student from Chicago. He wants to take easy courses, has low familiarity with the registration system, and prioritizes getting through enrollment quickly so he can plan his travels. His pain points: no professor ratings, confusing section availability, and prior knowledge from a different university system creating false expectations.

User Journey Map

Tracing the emotional arc of registration

We mapped Danny's emotional journey across four stages: Explore, Plan, Pre-Enroll, and Enroll. He starts curious but quickly becomes frustrated during planning, settling into mild uncertainty at pre-enrollment before feeling relieved once it is done. The dip during the Plan stage pinpointed the exact interaction to fix.

The sharpest frustration came during the Plan stage, when students had to cross-reference course times, professor options, and credit requirements across multiple disconnected screens with no live feedback.

02 - Low Fidelity Prototype

With clear pain points identified, we moved into rapid prototyping. The core hypothesis was a dual-panel layout: courses on one side, a live schedule on the other, updating as students make section selections. Two lo-fi directions were tested to see which mental model resonated.

Lo-Fi Wireframes

Dual-panel layout and visible schedule

Two directions were explored: a combined course-and-schedule view showing both panels at once, and a collapsible schedule that expands when a section is selected. Both directions shared one principle: time conflicts should appear on the schedule immediately, not after the student attempts to submit.

The collapsible approach tested better, as students preferred having the course list fill the screen by default and revealing the schedule only when needed.

Users didn't like the collapsing schedule. Having it hidden by default felt like losing information they needed. We also asked them to rank what actually mattered when picking classes, and those priorities directly shaped what we chose to surface in the high fidelity.

03 - Final Design

The final design unifies course browsing, section selection, and schedule visualization into a single interface. Pillar and Term filters narrow the catalog up front. A shopping cart model lets students plan before committing. The schedule panel updates in real time as sections are selected, and any time conflicts surface immediately with a transparent notice modal rather than a silent block.

For color, we gave local Singaporean students three options and a neutral brown came out the clear winner. Green and red were brought in as secondary accents for action buttons, and the multi-window layout was kept fully open so students could see everything at once without bouncing between screens.

Schedule View

Pillar filters, live calendar, and enrolled courses

The schedule view consolidates Pillar and Term filters, a live weekly calendar, and an enrolled course table into one screen. Students can filter to only see conflicting courses, toggle which course details appear on the calendar blocks, and remove or get more details on any enrolled class.

Course detail view: ratings, sections, friends, and live schedule panel

04 - Outcomes

After usability testing the redesigned interface, we ran follow-up surveys to measure perceived speed and clarity against the original system. The shift was significant across both measures.

Enrolment ease after redesign

Task completion speed after redesign

80% of users completed tasks faster than expected on the redesigned interface
90% rated the enrolment process as Easy or Very Easy after the redesign

The original system left 84% of students rating enrollment as difficult. After the redesign, 90% rated it easy or very easy. The shift was driven almost entirely by real-time schedule feedback and transparent conflict handling.
