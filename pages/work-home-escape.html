Work. Home. Escape.

Role Designer
Tools Adobe Illustrator
Type Poster, Illustration

About

These two posters were created as a series with a single premise: use word and image together to persuade. The design is intentionally versatile, built so the city name and color palette can shift while the structure stays intact. The same poster works for Los Angeles, New York, or anywhere else a transit system runs.

01 - The Series

Trains can take you to work, to home, and to escape. The poster makes that argument visually before the reader finishes the headline.

The series draws from the visual language of Green New Deal posters and mid-20th century transit propaganda, both of which used bold illustration to make public infrastructure feel exciting rather than utilitarian. The three words of the headline do the conceptual work: work, home, and escape represent the full range of where a train can take you, and together they make the case that public transit is not a last resort but a genuine choice. The bright, saturated colors are deliberate. They signal optimism and a future worth wanting. The gradients across the sky and buildings reference the sunset imagery common in futuristic illustration from the 1940s and 50s, when the idea of a technological future still felt like a promise. Each city gets its own palette but the same structure, so the series reads as unified while still feeling specific to place.
