Aptatio Analytics

Role Brand Designer
Duration 4 Months
Client Lehigh MBA Student

About

Aptatio Analytics is an app that uses predictive analysis and machine learning to optimize the patient/client team match, increasing a home healthcare agency's ability to earn the VBP payment reward. I was asked to join the team to help with their design needs.

While I was initially brought on to assist with UI, it became clear early on that the team's more pressing need was a cohesive brand identity. Without a clear visual language, any interface work would lack a foundation. I shifted focus to building the brand first, treating it as the groundwork that everything else would be built on top of.

01 - Initial Research

I began the project by working with the team to audit their existing system. They walked me through an AI-generated MVP that covered the basic user flow. It gave me a starting point for understanding what the product was trying to do, but reviewing it also made some gaps immediately apparent.

  • The logo and branding felt generic and did not communicate anything meaningful about what the company does or who it serves.
  • The MVP opened directly into the intake form with no landing page or context-setting screen, which would likely confuse new users unfamiliar with the product.

These observations shaped the priorities for the project. Before any screen could be redesigned, the team needed a brand that gave the product a clear identity and communicated trust to the healthcare professionals using it.

02 - Building the Brand

The first step was the branding. The team wanted an identity that reflected their goals and would hold up across professional contexts. I started by developing hand sketches exploring different directions and shared them with the team for feedback. They picked one direction, and from there we iterated together until we landed on a final mark that everyone felt confident in.

For the color, I chose a light blue to reflect trust and professionalism, two values that matter deeply in the healthcare space. The icon is shaped like a heartbeat pulse of the kind you would see on a patient monitor, making the connection to healthcare work immediately legible without being heavy-handed. I built out variations for different use cases so the brand could work whether it appeared on a dashboard header, a presentation deck, or a stakeholder email.

It was important to me that the logo feel clean but also approachable. While the company is data-driven, it should still feel personable and human so that the healthcare professionals using it can trust it.

03 - Design Applications

With the brand established, I was asked to create email templates for stakeholder communications. The team needed a format they could reuse for outreach and updates without having to design from scratch each time. The goal was something that carried the brand clearly, was easy to read at a glance, and kept the focus on the content rather than the design itself.

I kept the layout minimal: a clear header with the logo, a single-column body area with generous spacing, and a consistent footer. The typography and color usage followed the design system so that every email would feel like it came from the same place as the product.

Email template overview

I also redesigned the intake form website. One of the clearest problems from the audit was that users were dropped directly into the form with no orientation. I addressed this by adding a dashboard as a proper entry point, giving users a sense of where they were and what they were doing before any input was required. From there I focused on reducing input errors and improving clarity: a confirm step before submission, a visual representation of the patient/team match results so the output was easier to interpret, and larger input fields that were easier to interact with and less likely to cause mistakes.
