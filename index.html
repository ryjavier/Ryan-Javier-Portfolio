ABOUT

Three disciplines,
One Perspective.

Growing up in an immigrant household, I was often the family's unofficial IT person. I helped my grandparents post on Facebook, showed my mom how to AirDrop photos, and walked my aunt through setting up parental controls on YouTube. Over time I realized these weren't just technical hurdles. They were pain points created by products built for a specific kind of user, and my family didn't fit that mold. That realization shaped everything that followed.

I devoted my career to building technologies and interfaces that work for anybody. With an interdisciplinary background spanning Computer Science, Graphic Design, and Global Studies, I approach design as the practice of understanding systems and the people who interact with them. In other words, I make the complex feel simple.

Currently seeking Product Design roles where I can bring technical depth and human-centered thinking to teams solving real problems.
